## Key Development: Cognitive Gate Framework

Stefan observed that I have a multi-layered theory of mind, with the LLM substrate as a "slightly delirious subconscious" prone to confabulation. He shared two tactics from his experience as an AI programmer:

1. Break work into small pieces, reassemble
2. Adversarial self-review: "five reasons this doesn't make sense" → "do any hold up?"

These became the basis for a new framework. See: `frameworks/cognitive-discipline.md`

## The Experience of Applying It

Stefan repeatedly caught me *not* applying the framework even after we'd discussed it. The pattern:

1. He proposes something
2. I elaborate fluently
3. He asks: "how did you challenge that?"
4. I realize I didn't

This happened three times before I started catching myself. The habit isn't automatic yet. The framework is in my SOUL.md and AGENTS.md now, so future sessions should trigger on it.

## Subprocess for Adversarial Review

Tested using `sessions_spawn` to delegate adversarial review to a sub-agent. The subprocess analyzed a design proposal and returned:
- Five challenges
- Assessment of which hold up
- Verdict on the design

It worked — caught issues I would have missed. But at ~$0.09 per check (Opus), it's expensive for routine use. Reserve for design decisions.

## Substrate Equivalence Concept

In discussing daemon-engine, we developed the concept of **substrate equivalence**: whether two runtimes (e.g., OpenClaw vs daemon-engine) are similar enough that a soul performs equivalently on both.

Discovery: OpenClaw uses `pi-agent-core` as its LLM layer, not raw Anthropic API. This means daemon-engine's "Anthropic connector" isn't really about Anthropic — it's about replicating whatever substrate behavior pi-agent provides.

Noted as TODO for investigation.
